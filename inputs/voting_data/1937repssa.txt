Archie Galbraith Cameron (1895-1956): Elected 1934, 1937
Minister without portfolio 29 November 1937 to 7 November 1938
Postmaster-General 7 November 1938 to 26 April 1939
Minister for the Navy from 14 March 1940
Minister for Commerce from 14 March 1940
Leader of the Country Party from 13 September 1939
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Cameron was elected Leader of the Country Party following Page's 
resignation. In October 1940 he negotiated a new coalition with 
Menzies and became in effect deputy Prime Minister.

Albert Oliver Badman (1885-1977): Elected 1937
Born: 18 December 1885, Yacka, South Australia.
Career: Educated public schools. Wheat farmer, wheat-breeder.
Senate: Senator for South Australia 1931-37.
--------------------------------------------------------------------
1. The UAP agreed not to contest this seat (held by them 1931-37) in 
exchange for the nomination of the former member, McBride, to 
Badman's Senate vacancy.
2. Badman was one of a group of Country Party members (the others 
were Collins, Corser and Fadden) who dissociated themselves from 
Page's attack on Menzies in April 1939, and who also rejected 
Cameron's leadership of the Country Party in September.
