MACQUARIE, NSW                 39,271 enrolled, 35,535 (90.5%) voted
====================================================================
Central West NSW: Bathurst, Katoomba, Lithgow, Portland
--------------------------------------------------------------------
28 July 1951, following the death of Rt Hon Ben Chifley
--------------------------------------------------------------------
1951 two-party majority: ALP over Liberal 11.8
--------------------------------------------------------------------
William Blanchard                        3,853   10.9  
William Hannam                  Lib     12,356   35.1  (-03.1)
Anthony LUCHETTI                ALP     18,426   52.3  (-09.5)
Vernon Moffit                   CPA        575   01.6  
--------------------------------------------------------------------
325 (00.9%) informal                    35,210   11.0 e  00.8 to Lib
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Blanchard was an independent Labor candidate. His candidacy was a 
protest at Luchetti's selection as Chifley's successor. Luchetti had 
been the ALP (NSW) candidate in Macquarie in 1931 and 1934, his 
preferences defeating Chifley in 1931. 
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Anthony Sylvester Luchetti (1904-84): Elected 1951 by
Born: 27 May 1904, Lowther, NSW.
Career: Educated Catholic schools. Many jobs including mining and 
journalism. Lithgow City Council. NSW ALP Executive 1929-31.

There were ten by-elections during this Parliament, the highest 
number during any Parliament. This followed two Parliaments (1946-49
and 1949-51 in which there were no by-elections.
