COMMONWEALTH OF AUSTRALIA
LEGISLATIVE ELECTION OF 15 SEPTEMBER 1934
====================================================================

Parties
--------------------------------------------------------------------
* ALP - Australian Labor Party
* CP - Country Party
* UAP – United Australia Party

BY-ELECTIONS 
====================================================================

NEWCASTLE, NSW                 56,282 enrolled, 52,719 (93.7%) voted
====================================================================
North Central NSW: Hamilton, Mayfield, Newcastle, Wickham
--------------------------------------------------------------------
1 June 1935, following the death of David Watkins
--------------------------------------------------------------------
1934 two-party majority: ALP over ALP (NSW) 08.1 e
--------------------------------------------------------------------
James Smith                     ALPN    22,135   42.7  (+08.2)
Hilton Sykes                    SC       4,302   08.3
David Watkins                   ALP     25,374   49.0  (-08.2)
--------------------------------------------------------------------
908 (01.7%) informal                    51,811
--------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Smith was NSW MLA for Hamilton 1928-30.
2. The combined ALP and ALP (NSW) vote was 91.7%, the same as the 
91.7% polled by the two parties in 1931. Again, the ALP vote was 
boosted by the absence of a conservative candidate.
--------------------------------------------------------------------

2nd count: Sykes's 4,302 votes distributed
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Smith              1,809 (42.1)         23,944   46.2
WATKINS            2,493 (57.9)         27,867   53.8
--------------------------------------------------------------------
>                  4,302                51,811   03.8
--------------------------------------------------------------------
There was a swing of 4.3% from the ALP to the ALP (NSW).
--------------------------------------------------------------------
David Oliver Watkins (1896-1971): Elected 1935 By
Born: 10 July 1896, Wallsend, NSW.
Career: Educated public schools, Newcastle. Storeman. Military 
Service 1915-17. Son of Hon David Watkins MP.

GWYDIR, NSW                    54,791 enrolled, 49,476 (90.3%) voted
====================================================================
Northern NSW: Inverell, Moree, Mudgee, Quirindi
--------------------------------------------------------------------
8 May 1937, following the resignation of Hon Aubrey Abbott
--------------------------------------------------------------------
1934 two-party majority: Country Party over ALP (NSW) 04.5
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Ernest Batchelor                CP      13,168   27.0
Oliver Milling                  CP       8,155   16.7
Geoffrey Moore                  CP       1,963   04.0
William SCULLY                  ALP     25,551   52.3  (+44.2)
--------------------------------------------------------------------
639 (01.3%) informal                    48,837   04.3 e  08.8 to ALP
--------------------------------------------------------------------
1. The combined Country Party vote was 47.7%, a decrease of 4.8% 
from the 52.5% polled by one Country Party candidate at the 1934 
general election.
2. The ALP vote of 52.3% was an increase of 7.6% from the combined 
vote of 44.7% polled by the ALP and the ALP (NSW) in 1934. The huge 
increase in the ALP vote was the result of the reunification of the 
NSW and federal ALP.
3. The ALP majority over the Country Party is compared with the 
Country Party majority over the ALP (NSW) at the 1934 general 
election.
--------------------------------------------------------------------
William James Scully (1890-1966): Elected 1937 By
Born: 1 February 1890, Bective, NSW.
Career: No formal education. Grew up on station, farmworker. Road 
contractor, Tamworth. Horse-owner. Tamworth Council.
State politics: NSW MLA for Namoi 1923-32.
