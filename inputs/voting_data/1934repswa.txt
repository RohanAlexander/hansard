--------------------------------------------------------------------
1,438 (02.9%) informal                  48,874
--------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Cardell-Oliver (later Dame Florence) was WA MLA for Subiaco 1936-
56. She was the first woman to be a Cabinet minister in Australia 
(in 1947). She was also the first woman endorsed by a major party 
for a winnable seat in the House of Representatives.
2. Curtin was member for Fremantle 1928-31.

--------------------------------------------------------------------
John Curtin (1885-1945): Elected 1928, 1929. Defeated 1931. Elected 
1934
Leader of the Opposition from 1 October 1935
Leader of the Australian Labor Party from 1 October 1935
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Curtin was elected leader of the ALP following the retirement of 
Scullin. His accession to the leadership was mainly due to the fact 
that he had opposed the Premiers' Plan in 1931, and led directly to 
the reunification of the federal ALP and the NSW Branch in March 
1936.
