>  John Thomas Lang (1876-1975): Elected 1946 
Born: 21 December 1876, Sydney.
Career: Primary education. Worked various jobs, accountancy firm. 
Estate agent, Sydney. Auburn Municipal Council. Newspaper publisher.
State politics: NSW MLA for Granville 1913-20, Parramatta 1920-27, 
Auburn 1927-43, 1943-46. Colonial Treasurer 1920-22, 1925-27, 1930-
32. Premier 1925-27, 1930-32. Dismissed by Governor 1932 for 
breaching Audit Act. Leader of the Opposition 1923-25, 1927-30, 
1932-39. Led NSW ALP in split with federal party, 1931-36. Expelled 
from ALP 1940, readmitted 1941, finally expelled 1943. Leader of 
Australian Labor Party (Non-Communist) 1940-41, 1943-51.
Lang contested Blaxland in 1949.
